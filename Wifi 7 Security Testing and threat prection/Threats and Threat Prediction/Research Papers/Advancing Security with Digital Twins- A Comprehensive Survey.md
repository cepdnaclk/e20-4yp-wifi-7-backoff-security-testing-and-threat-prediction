- **Title:** Advancing Security with Digital Twins: A Comprehensive Survey
    
- **Authors:** Blessing Airehenbuwa, Touseef Hasan, Souvika Sarkar, and Ujjwal Guin
    
- **Year:** 2025
    
- **Summary:** This survey paper proposes using **Digital Twin (DT)** technology as a powerful tool for hardware and system security. A DT is a dynamic, virtual replica of a physical object or system that is updated with real-time data. The paper argues that DTs can be used to monitor, simulate, and analyze complex systems (like IoT networks or cyber-physical systems) to detect threats such as counterfeiting, tampering, side-channel attacks, and intrusions. It also looks ahead to integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) with DTs for even smarter security analysis.
    
- **Important Points:**
    
    - A Digital Twin is a "living" virtual model of a physical system.
        
    - DTs can provide end-to-end visibility and continuous verification of a system's integrity.
        
    - DTs can be used for security functions like predictive maintenance, fault detection, and anomaly detection.
        
    - The paper suggests combining LLMs with DTs to enhance reasoning and decision-making for security.
        
- **New Technologies, Findings, and Important Aspects:**
    
    - **New Methodology:** Applying Digital Twin technology as a framework for security testing and threat prediction.
        
    - **Relevance to Wi-Fi 7 Security Testing:** This paper provides a _methodology_ for testing. A complex Wi-Fi 7 MLO network is a perfect candidate for a DT. Testers could create a DT of a Wi-Fi 7 AP or an entire network. This DT would be a high-fidelity simulation fed real-time data from the physical AP. Testers could then safely launch attacks (like the AML or phishing attacks described in the other papers) against the _Digital Twin_ to test defenses, predict impacts, and find vulnerabilities without disrupting the live network.
        
    - **Relevance to Threat Prediction:** This is a powerful tool for threat prediction. By having a "living" DT of a Wi-Fi 7 network, a security team can "war-game" novel threats. For example: "What if an attacker combines a 6 GHz jammer with a 'Trident'-style phishing attack on the 5 GHz link?" This complex, multi-link scenario can be simulated on the DT to predict the outcome and build defenses _before_ the attack is ever seen in the wild.
