## ‚öôÔ∏è **Evolution Highlights**

### **Wi-Fi 1‚Äì3 (802.11b/a/g): The Foundation Era**

- Basic single-link, single-band communication.
    
- Introduced wireless connectivity to homes and enterprises.
    
- Limited bandwidth and interference management.
    

---

### **Wi-Fi 4 (802.11n): The MIMO Revolution**

- Introduced **MIMO** (multiple antennas for parallel data streams).
    
- First to use **both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz** bands.
    
- Allowed **channel bonding** (40 MHz) for higher throughput.
    

---

### **Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac): Speed and Capacity Boost**

- Moved fully to **5 GHz**.
    
- Added **MU-MIMO** for simultaneous multi-user transmissions.
    
- Adopted **beamforming** to focus signal power toward specific devices.
    
- Modulation increased to **256-QAM**, enhancing spectral efficiency.
    

---

### **Wi-Fi 6 / 6E (802.11ax): Efficiency and Density**

- Focused on **network efficiency**, not just speed.
    
- Introduced **OFDMA** (splits channel into subcarriers for multiple users).
    
- **MU-MIMO in both uplink and downlink.**
    
- **TWT** for power saving ‚Äî key for IoT devices.
    
- **6E extension** opened the 6 GHz spectrum (14 additional 80 MHz channels or 7√ó160 MHz channels).
    

---

### **Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be): The MLO and Extreme Throughput Era**

- Designed for **ultra-low latency**, **high throughput**, and **deterministic reliability** for future applications like:
    
    - XR/VR, AR, 8K/16K streaming
        
    - Industrial IoT, cloud gaming, AI-driven wireless networks
        
- **Key Innovations:**
    
    - **Multi-Link Operation (MLO):** simultaneous use of multiple bands/links (2.4, 5, 6 GHz).
        
    - **320 MHz Channel Bandwidth** ‚Äî doubling from Wi-Fi 6.
        
    - **4096-QAM (4K-QAM):** higher data per symbol.
        
    - **Multi-RU allocation:** flexible resource sharing.
        
    - **HARQ (Hybrid ARQ):** reliability improvement through re-transmission combining.
        
    - **Low-latency scheduling and preamble puncturing.**
